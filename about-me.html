About Me

Projects

To my friends, I’m the kind guy. To my customers and coworkers, I’m the teacher. To my enemies...I’m still awkwardly kind. It’s a hard habit to break, but one that helps me fit in well with a multitude of teams and environments. I will always be the guy who is willing to talk about almost any topic that might come time someone’s mind, willing to find every chance to read a book and bursting out in song at the drop of a hat. At the same time, I will always be the person who will go above and beyond to make sure things get done right and committed to making sure that everyone feels like they have a part.

After 5 years of medical research working in labs that filled up a whole floor to a shoebox in the basement, all with low to no human interaction, I decided I needed a change. I wanted to find something that would still allow me to make a difference, but would allow me to have a high amount of collaboration with others to create more tangible results. This lead to me working for 2 ½ years as an Immigration Program Manager overseeing $10+ million in business revenue as we helped people have legal work authorization and gave companies across the country access to that talent. While this role change gave me the collaboration I craved along with quicker results, it was still missing something. In the end, I lost the ability to create, which was one of the things that I loved about research.

With my immigration work, I ended up interacting a lot with people who worked on all sides of the software development spectrum. These conversations and their passion for the work that they were about to build and create turned on a lightbulb for me.

Web development is like the perfect blend of languages and math. I bundle things together just right and something beautiful comes out. At the same time, If I throw things together just to see what happens, you continue to discover something new. All of this happens while I ultimately create something that I can share immediately. At the same time, it requires high amounts of collaboration as you work with others to put everything together.

It sounded perfect, so I joined a full-stack Bootcamp where I’ve grown my appreciation for languages like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Now I strive to continue learning more and refining my skills. I’m excited to see how I can continue to create something beautiful and useful for everyone around me.
